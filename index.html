WHY I LOVE HORROR

Welcome to my page, HORROR fans!!! Thanks for stopping in. I'm excited to take you on a little journey through space and time and TERROR - as only a true horror fan can! As long as I could remember, I always loved to be scared in a peculiar fashion. You know --glued to the TV, fingers splayed across my face strategically spread out so I could still see what was happening and at the same time protect myself from being completely freaked out by the jump scare (thereby defeating the whole effect of the jump scare --DUH!). That never worked anyway. So here I am today with a lot of memories of a lot of scares.
Keep in mind, my history with horror began when I was a kid (about 8) so some of the movies I'll talk about will seem laughable rather than horrific, but nevertheless, they are indelibly etched in my brain as HORROR CLASSICS. Sure the advent of CGI made special effects seem more realistic (So?!), but it's those rough hewn latex and corn syrup blood and guts that will stick with me forever. So, take a few minutes to peek inside my world -- if you dare (queue "creepy old guy laugh")!

Doug Bradley as "Pinhead" in Hellraiser .

AHHH! The Dead Walk The Earth!!!

First up: DAWN OF THE DEAD (1978)! This is the movie from which we get that quote which makes the blood run cold. You know, when Ken Foree's character says: "When there is no more room in Hell, the dead will walk the Earth."

Deep.

So this movie was the 2nd installment in George A. Romero's classic "-Of The Dead Series". The scary the scary thing about this film for me was that most of it was set in a mall. I loved going to the mall and me and my mom would often be at Crestwood Mall on Saturday evenings until they closed. So many times I would look back and watch the mall attendant lock the doors as we walked to our car and imagine they would wander the huge empty shopping center trying on clothes and spraying cologne samples after all the customers had gone. Well, Dawn Of The Dead totally ruined that playful rumination for me 😱. I loved it!
This movie served as my matriculation into the world of ZOMBIES! From then on, I was obsessed. I learned the history of zombies from the slow-moving, chomping zombies of pre-Romero films to the tweaky rage zombies of Danny Boyle's 28 Days Later (2002). What I found was a rich history of the undead. Still to this day, I wonder if Macy's or IKEA will be my final refuge when the zombie apocalypse happens.

It's A Big Red Gooey Gelatenous Mass That Eats People!

You guessed it! My next favorite is THE BLOB(1958) !!! Holy carnivorous Jell-O ®, Batman! What more can I say? So, The Blob is about this red substances that crashes to Earth in a meteor. On impact, the meteor splits open and this dark red goo oozes out. From there it gets kinda gross (by the 1958 standard of gross, of course) because this goo just instantly digests whatever it touches. It eats an old man and then a doctor. By then, it is big and strong enough to take on the town.Steve McQueen is the main character who tries to rescue the town from the jello monster. The problem is no one believes him until it's too late -- for them. Suprisingly, this movie did NOT ruin jello for me. It definitely made it more interesting. The other cool thing about this film is it's 1988 remake where actor Kevin Dillon is the main character trying to warn the unsuspecting citizens of Arborville, California.
While I still LOVE jello, don't EVER ask me to watch a meteor shower!

Look! More Dead People!

Last, but certainly not least is the first horror movie to actually give me nightmares -- 1981's The Evil Dead (don't laugh...I was 10). So a group group of college students at cabin in the woods find these tapes which they play on a reel-to-reel. It's some creepy guy saying what sounds like an incantation in a foreign language. Sure enough, the audio wakes up something wicked in the woods. Something evil. As the night wears on, the students undergo grusome changes as they are alternately possessed by demons. The main character "Ash" (feverishly portrayed by a very young Bruce Campbell), must battle the demons to save his friends and later, himself. This film has everything from its dark and creepy atmosphere, to a gruesome assault by foliage. The image that made me want to run out of the theater was the one where a demon-possessed woman tries to escape from the cellar. Needless to say, vacationing at a cabin in the woods still sounds like a terrible idea to me. Especially if said cabin happens to have a cellar. If you're done laughing, I'll have you know that this movie scared me more than the original Friday The 13TH film (1980).

Well, thanks again for checking out my page. Hope you've enjoyed this tiny little romp through horror history. Until next time, don't eat jello in the woods behind the old mall. Bye, Horror Fans!
