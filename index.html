WHY I LOVE HORROR

Welcome to my page, HORROR fans!!! Thanks for stopping in. I'm excited to take on a little journey through space and time and TERROR - as only a true horror fan can! As long as I could remember, I always loved to be scared in a peculiar fashion. You know --glued to the TV, fingers splayed across my face strategically spread out so I could still see what was happening and at the same time protect myself from being completely freaked out by the jump scare (thereby defeating the whole effect of the jump scare --DUH!). That never worked anyway. So here I am today with a lot of memories of a lot of scares.
Keep in mind, my history with horror began when I was a kid (about 8) so some of the movies I'll talk about will seem laughable rather than horrific, but nevertheless, they are indelibly etched in my brain as HORROR CLASSICS. Sure the advent of CGI made special effects seem more realistic (So?!)

A Bird in the Hand

The bird exhibit was next and outside there was a whole flock of brightly colored Flamingos. The boys posed for the camera and as Tristan walked along the fence to see what all he could, Chance watched as the birds wondered about.

We then went inside and had was greated with a great view of the penguins. They dove into the water and spashed about. Tristan was able to get close and they followed him along the acrylic wall. He really enjoyed being able to interact with the them.






Sea Lions, Tigers and Bears "Oh My!"

The Seals and SeaLions attracted the boys next. Tristan ran between the wondows watching them glide by as Chance watched and wondered how they did not run into the wall with as close as they were comming to it. When we went inside Chancellor lifted Tristan up to get a vetter view but all Tristan was interested in was trying to get to the ceiling. Chance obliged and held him as high as he could as the seals and sea lions came close. This was definitely the exibit both boys loved most.





And Now For The Rest....

Next we headed for the Elephants and there we found a small statue for Tristan to climb on. Chancellor helped. Tristan was amazed at the size of the pacaderms but soon lost interest when he realised he could not get close to them.



The Tapir was enjoying the cool sprinkle and both boys watched him as he pranced about.
